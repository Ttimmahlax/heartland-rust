## What Is The ALTAIR Enlighten Award?

The ALTAIR Enlighten Award is a globally recognized accolade that celebrates groundbreaking advancements in automotive sustainability and lightweighting. Presented annually in collaboration with the Center for Automotive Research (CAR), it highlights innovations that reduce carbon footprints, conserve energy and water, and promote material recycling.

Over its 12-year history, the award has become a prestigious benchmark, earning a spot among EV Magazine’s top 10 sustainability awards in 2024. It attracts entries from industry leaders, startups, and academia, showcasing cutting-edge technologies that shape the future of mobility.

The award’s notoriety stems from its rigorous evaluation by an independent panel of automotive experts, ensuring credibility and impartiality. Winners, such as BMW, Toyota, and DuPont, gain significant visibility, joining an elite group of trailblazers driving sustainable automotive solutions. The ceremony, held at CAR’s Management Briefing Seminars, garners attention from policymakers, engineers, and media worldwide. Its media partnerships with outlets like SAE International and Automobil Industrie amplify its reach, cementing its status as a coveted prize in the automotive sector.

## What Is Low Carbon Polyamide 6 from BASF?

BASF, in collaboration with Heartland, has developed a low carbon polyamide 6 (PA6), branded as Ultramid®, reinforced with hemp fiber to meet stringent automotive specifications. This innovative material integrates Heartland’s Imperium hemp fiber additives, which reduce the carbon footprint of plastics while maintaining performance.

The hemp-reinforced PA6 offers a sustainable alternative to traditional fillers like glass fiber, achieving a lighter composite with the same performance. It provides enhanced mechanical properties, including high tensile strength and impact resistance, suitable for automotive exterior components.

The material’s carbon-negative profile stems from hemp’s ability to sequester 1.4 tons of CO₂ per ton processed, offsetting emissions from production. BASF’s expertise in polyamide compounding ensures the hemp fiber integrates seamlessly without requiring retooling, making it a drop-in solution for manufacturers. This partnership, supported by BASF’s Chemovator investment and North America Open Research Alliance, aligns with industry goals to reduce scope 3 emissions.

The resulting PA6 meets automotive standards for durability and processability, positioning it as a viable, eco-friendly option for sustainable vehicle production.

## What Does This Mean For Manufacturers?

For the first time, manufacturers can access BASF’s low-carbon-emission polyamide 6 (PA6), Ultramid®, reinforced with Heartland’s hemp fiber, offering a sustainable alternative to traditional materials. This breakthrough allows automotive manufacturers to reduce scope 3 emissions without compromising performance, as the hemp-based PA6 meets existing specifications for strength and durability.

The material’s lower density compared to glass fiber-reinforced composites enables lighter vehicle components, improving fuel efficiency and supporting electrification goals. Manufacturers benefit from a drop-in solution that requires no retooling, streamlining integration into existing production lines.

Partnerships like Magna’s adoption of this PA6 demonstrate its viability for high-volume production. Ultimately, this advancement empowers manufacturers to align with global decarbonization efforts while maintaining cost-effective, high-quality output.
