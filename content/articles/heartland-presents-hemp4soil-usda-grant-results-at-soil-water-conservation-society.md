## What Is The Hemp4Soil USDA Grant?

The Hemp4Soil Grant was awarded to Heartland in 2022 to explore the integration of hemp fiber in conventional crop rotations throughout the United States. The farmers involved in this program raise corn, soybeans, wheat, and other commodity crops, each of them began implementing hemp fiber into the rotation to understand the impact on soil health, root structure, carbon sequestration, and income potential.

The goal was to understand if it is viable to introduce hemp fiber as an additional cash crop in a farmer's crop rotation, and identify any changes to soil health, and quantify any carbon sequestration capabilities of regenerative farming practices being introduced with the hemp fiber crop.

## What Was The Result Of The Hemp4Soil Grant?

The results of the Hemp4soil grant are very definitive. We found that with regenerative farming practices, there was a night-and-day difference in soil respiration and density. The improvement of soil respiration created an environment where organic matter could thrive, whereas conventional farming had almost no organic life. The organic matter increases translate directly to improved fungi and bacteria activity in the soil, ultimately increasing our money maker, Nitrogen.

The changes in farming practices from conventional to the Engineering Earth method, using high speed discs for tillage, cover cropping, and tracks instead of tires, was at the cornerstone of increasing organic matter in the soil.

This shift of practices led to an economic benefit to the farmer, reducing their operating time in the field, reducing input costs to raise the same crop, and ultimately putting more money in their pockets.

## What Do The Hemp4Soil Results Mean For Farmers?

The farming community has been going back and forth on the opportunity to convert from conventional farming to regenerative farming. Our Hemp4Soil program showcases that it is more economically viable to regeneratively farm than it is to conventionally farm. What this means over time is that if you do not make the change, you will go out of business. Farming is a business of low profitability for high output, and every edge a farmer can get will allow them to pocket more money compared to the competition.

When Private Equity sees the economic benefit of regenerative farming, their investment firms will begin mandating conversions to remain competitive, and we expect to see a major shift from conventional farming.

As a small farmer, this is an opportunity to jump ahead of the competition and build a competitive advantage over big business.

## Where Can I Get Involved?

The Heartland team has put together a set of farming guidelines for [Engineering Earth](/engineering-earth/), focusing on the operational shifts that improve soil organic matter and carbon sequestration. We're working with partners to develop opportunities for farmers to capitalize on carbon credits generated from farming.

In addition to that, we're a buyer of hemp fiber and grain products from farmers throughout the United States, and would love to engage with the community more to build our marketplace.
