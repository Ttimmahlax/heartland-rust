## What is hurd milled into a powder and what are the benefits and challenges?

The hemp stalk can be separated into two parts. The fiber is used as a reinforcement agent and the hurd can be used as a filler. These fillers can be milled to meet specifications, but do not provide any performance benefits.

### Pros

#### Low Cost

Hemp fiber is a low-cost alternative to traditional materials used throughout manufacturing, including talc, calcium carbonate, and glass fiber in plastic, carbon black in rubber, and other minerals in construction. The most important cost reduction opportunity is driven by the distance of your processor to your facility.

#### Carbon Negative

In addition to their strength advantages, [hemp fibers are also celebrated for their eco-friendly nature](http://carbon-report.com). Hemp is a highly sustainable crop that requires minimal pesticides and herbicides. It grows rapidly, has a high yield per acre, and its cultivation helps improve soil health. Choosing hemp fibers over synthetic materials can contribute to a more sustainable and environmentally friendly approach to production.

It is important to work with an industrial hemp supplier that has an [LCA](/lca) on their products. This will enable your business to make public claims about your hard work decarbonizing products.

### Cons

#### How It Is Processed

Different processing methods to separate the fiber and the hurd from the hemp stalk can cause downstream performance problems in different composite materials, including plastic, rubber, and construction products. It is important to understand what methods your processors are using and to avoid decortication for use cases that are not textiles.

#### Handling & Processing

Hemp fibers have a relatively low density, meaning they occupy a larger volume compared to their mass. This bulkiness can make it challenging to handle and feed the fibers smoothly into equipment such as conveyors, hoppers, or processing machinery. The poor flowability of hemp fibers can result in bridging, clogging, or uneven feeding, leading to interruptions in the production process.

Due to their low density and inherent interlocking nature, hemp fibers can have a tendency to entangle or form clumps during handling and feeding processes. This can further exacerbate the feeding challenges by causing blockages, uneven distribution, or uneven processing of the fibers.

#### Fire & Dust Concerns

During processing or handling, hemp fibers can generate dust particles, which may pose inhalation risks or potential irritants to the eyes, nose, and throat. This is particularly relevant in industrial settings where large quantities of hemp fibers are processed or manipulated. To minimize dust-related issues, proper ventilation, dust control measures, and personal protective equipment (such as masks and goggles) should be employed where necessary. Like other natural fibers, hemp fibers can burn when exposed to an open flame or high heat. The flammability of hemp fibers can be influenced by factors such as fiber density, moisture content, and chemical treatments.

#### Logistics

As hemp fibers occupy a larger volume relative to their weight, it can result in higher shipping costs compared to denser materials. The transportation cost per unit of volume may be higher, leading to potential cost challenges, especially for long-distance or bulk shipments.

Unfunctionalized hemp fibers require special handling and packaging considerations due to their low density. Proper packaging, such as compressed bales or containers, can help reduce volume and optimize transportation efficiency. However, additional equipment or labor may be needed for effective handling and packaging, which can contribute to overall logistics costs.

## **What Makes One Hemp Product Different Than Another?**

### Different Genetics of the Hemp Fiber

The strength of hemp fibers is a crucial performance factor. Different hemp genetics can result in varying tensile strength, impacting their ability to withstand stretching and pulling forces. Stronger fibers are desirable in applications where durability is critical, such as in industrial textiles, construction materials, and automotive components.

### Cost-Effective Processing Methods

The standard method to process hemp fiber today is by decorticating the stalk. This process is known to be slow and expensive, potentially damaging the fibers and hurd. Newer methods have become available that can process the stalk more effectively at much faster rates, reducing costs. These methods are utilized by [Heartland](http://Heartland.io) before their functionalization process.

### Hemp Fiber Functionalization

Hemp fiber bonds with hemp fiber really well. When you look to bond hemp fiber to a new product, it has a much harder time staying together. It is important that the hemp fiber you're purchasing is functionalized for your use case. This is a specified process done by a few hemp fiber processors and distributors for their customers. Unfunctionalized fiber may cause failure in different products.

### Processor Location

Hemp fiber is extremely lightweight and low-cost. For the same reason you do not ship plastic or concrete far distances, you do not want to ship hemp fiber far distances. It is important to work with a supplier that intends to operate locally to your operations.
