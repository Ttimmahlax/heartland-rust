## What Is The Hemp Fiber and Grain Association?

The Hemp Fiber and Grain Association (HFGA) is a dedicated organization that serves as a key resource for American farmers interested in growing industrial hemp specifically for fiber and grain production. Founded to support the resurgence of hemp as a viable commodity crop following its legalization through the 2018 Farm Bill, the HFGA focuses on providing practical guidance to make hemp cultivation accessible and profitable.

It offers farmers access to reliable seed partners, proven best practices for planting, harvesting, and crop management, as well as connections to buyers who purchase hemp fiber and grain. The association emphasizes building a clear roadmap that simplifies the process of starting and scaling industrial hemp operations, from field preparation to market sales.

By facilitating direct links between growers and processors or exporters, HFGA helps eliminate barriers and reduces risks for new and experienced farmers alike. It tracks and shares industry data, such as contracted acres, tons of fiber and grain produced, and pricing premiums compared to traditional crops like soybeans.

The organization also provides updates on local policies, regulations, and market trends to ensure compliance and informed decision-making. Through its efforts, HFGA aims to drive rural economic growth by promoting hemp as a sustainable, high-value alternative or rotation crop. It operates with no fees for its farming community, positioning itself as a premier partner in expanding domestic production and even export opportunities for U.S. hemp fiber and grain.

## Why Is It Important For Heartland To Partner With HFGA?

The partnership between Heartland Industries and the Hemp Fiber and Grain Association is crucial for building a reliable and scalable U.S. hemp supply chain. By collaborating with HFGA, Heartland is helping educate a network of farmers on best practices for planting and harvesting, and regional crop rotation strategies that minimize risk and integrate hemp seamlessly into traditional rotations like corn, wheat, and soy.

This alliance helps Heartland secure consistent, high-quality fiber supplies from American growers, supporting its mission to provide manufacturers with carbon-negative, high-performance materials while driving profitability for farmers through vetted buyers and premium opportunities.

Ultimately, the partnership accelerates the growth of the non-CBD industrial hemp sector, fosters regenerative agriculture, and positions both organizations as leaders in expanding domestic production and export potential for hemp fiber and grain.
