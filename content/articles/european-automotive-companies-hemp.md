Although hemp has been around for over 10,000 years, Americans make it out to be a new, novel, and revolutionary material. Manufacturers in America are just starting to become familiarized with hemp’s properties. But, in Europe, many manufacturers have been using hemp for decades.

American consumers can see hemp embedded into all different types of European parts. But, when it comes to sourcing American goods made from hemp, consumers are only left with one solution: importing.

Industrial hemp has faced roadblock after roadblock here in America. The battle was agriculture vs industry. Unfortunately, agriculture faced challenges from multiple multi-billion dollar interests during the ‘war on drugs.’ This created two main pieces of legislation.

- 1937 Marijuana Tax Act.

- Controlled Substances Act of 1970.

These are just a couple of the obstacles hemp had to climb through just to get to the starting line.

But, now that the US government passed the [2018 Farm Bill](https://www.fda.gov/news-events/congressional-testimony/hemp-production-and-2018-farm-bill-07252019), hemp is now 100% federally legal.

This unlocks the floodgates of opportunity that manufacturers have been waiting for.

## **So, where is there an opportunity for hemp fiber to decarbonize manufacturing?**

I’m glad you asked.

We can look at global markets that utilize hemp in textiles, building materials, and plastics.

But, the use case where hemp has been proven to be an effective solution time and time again is the automotive industry.

How do we know this? Because European automakers have all adopted hemp in several parts of their vehicles.

Here are 6 European auto manufacturers and some of the parts of their vehicles that have leveraged hemp-based additives over the years.

- [Volvo](https://www.media.volvocars.com/global/en-gb/media/pressreleases/5084) – Uses natural fibers to cut the use of glass fiber and reduce weight.

- [Porche](https://www.compositesworld.com/news/porsche-production-car-produced-using-natural-fiber-composites) – The 718 Cayman GT 4 Club Sport utilizes hemp.

- [Peugeot](https://europe.autonews.com/article/20170802/COPY/308049983/faurecia-turns-to-hemp-to-reduce-reliance-on-plastic-parts) – The Peugeot 308 saw 25% weight savings compared to traditional plastics.

## European auto manufacturers have proven hemp benefits:

### **Hemp fiber increases strength**

Hemp fibers have a stronger tensile strength than glass fibers, allowing manufacturers to use fewer products to accomplish the same results.

### **Hemp fiber reduces weight**

Hemp fibers have a lower molecular weight than glass fibers, which means when manufacturers use the same amount of fibers, they can reduce the weight of their end product by up to 44%.

### **Hemp fiber reduces cost**

Glass fibers come with a hefty price increase over virgin plastic material with traditional fillers. Hemp fibers reduce the cost of goods sold (COGS) while maintaining superior attributes.

### **Hemp fiber is carbon negative**

The growing and processing of hemp fibers remove 2.85 tons of carbon from the atmosphere for every 1 ton grown. Glass fibers have a net negative impact on the environment that has been overlooked because of petroleum interests driving its adoption.

This value proposition can be easily attained by companies that manufacture automotive parts.

## Hemp Fiber Filled Polypropylene & Hemp Fiber Filled Polyethelyene for Automotive

Two of the most common plastic resins that hemp fiber adds value to are PP and PE.

These are two of the most common plastics used in automotive and provide lightweight, low-cost solutions to drive the world forward.

Hemp fiber further reduces the cost, weight, and carbon footprint of these plastics without compromising the performance of the plastic parts.

One of the capabilities that automotive companies are excited about is [lightweighting](/News/industrial-hemp-supply-chain-unlocks-the-future-of-mobility-through-lightweighting/).

Lighter cars accelerate faster, go further, carry more weight, produce lower emissions, and get increased miles per gallon.
