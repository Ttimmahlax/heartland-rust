## The Impacts of AI on Cloud Services & Data Privacy

The rapid integration of artificial intelligence into every layer of business is transforming cloud services — but not always for the better. While AI unlocks unprecedented capabilities in data analysis, automation, and customer insights, it also amplifies long-standing privacy vulnerabilities and introduces new ones at scale.

## What Makes Sense To Decloud VS Keeping Online?

In an era where [AI is supercharging data usage](https://www.kiteworks.com/cybersecurity-risk-management/ai-data-security-crisis-shadow-ai-governance-strategies-2026/), the risks of centralized cloud storage have escalated sharply. Generative AI usage has tripled in recent years, while data policy violations have doubled. Shadow AI (unauthorized use of tools like public LLMs) and agentic AI systems create new insider threats, with machine identities outnumbering human ones by ratios as high as 100:1.

## How We're Planning on Declouding Customer Data Within Heartland

The modern world relies heavily on [cloud services](/sustainability-news/collecting-farming-data-to-standardize-hemp-insurance-lending/), including Heartland. Everything we do is embedded in the cloud, and glaring privacy concerns are arising with AI. We believe that owning the data you create is fundamental to the value creation process, but we are not willing to compromise on the quality of services or availability and access to data.

This is a phased approach. It started with personal declouding tools like [mata.network](http://mata.network), and has evolved into simple services first, then more complex services listed below.

### Web Assets

Heartland has already begun migrating many web assets off WordPress and conventional hosting services. The goal has been to improve speed, SEO validation, and security while protecting our fundamental data. This website is our last web asset that needs to be migrated due to complexity and uptime requirements.

### Web Services

Heartland uses hundreds of web services to manage files, identify customers, enrich data, and so much more. Many of these are impossible to decloud, but select tools can be.

### Long Term Goal

Heartland develops IP across every aspect of our business, from farm processes and technologies through trade routes and customers. Although we open-source or provide many pieces of data for free to our industry, some things we keep close to our chest. Our focus will be on improving performance, security, and access to our data through this process while maintaining ownership.
