Heartland Industries is thrilled to announce our developments in India to support Indian yarn and fabric mills with a consistent and reliable supply chain of hemp fiber

## The Importance Of Hemp Yarn and Hemp Fabric in India

Historically, textile-grade hemp fiber has been almost exclusively sourced from China, with smaller contributions from Europe (France, Belgium, Poland). In many major textile-producing regions, regulatory restrictions on growing or importing hemp have limited its adoption, keeping the industry dependent on a narrow supply base.

Recent regulatory shifts worldwide are changing this landscape, increasing access to hemp fiber and creating opportunities for  US suppliers to gain market share. As demand surges for eco-friendly alternatives to cotton and synthetics, U.S.-grown hemp offers cost stability, shorter supply chains, and superior sustainability credentials.

Hemp requires up to 95% less water than cotton, needs no pesticides, and enriches soil health through crop rotation, making it a regenerative powerhouse in an industry facing mounting pressure to reduce environmental impact.

## What Products Can Be Made With Hemp Fiber?

While hemp fiber typically costs more than conventional cotton upfront, its superior performance characteristics justify the investment in premium applications:

- **Activewear**: Hemp fiber is almost as strong as nylon and pairs well with spandex to provide a pesticide free, microplastic free, activewear with the same soft feel.

- **Bedding & Towels**: Hemp fiber can replace linen in bedding and towel products, providing exceptional strength and drying performance. Linen is going through a shortage right now increasing costs, making hemp fiber a perfect alternative.

- **Denim**: Hemp fiber is resistant to tearing, bacteria, mold, salt water, and UV rays, keeping denim fresher longer while feeling even softer than cotton.

- **Furniture**: Hemp fiber is soft, strong, and UV resistence to last the ages on any furniture. Additionally, functional fabrics out perform synthetics in key long term uses.

- **Functional Fabrics**: Hemp fiber reinforces conventional fabrics converted into fake leather or coated fabrics. These applications across canvas and bags are a great low cost and sustainable alternative.

These attributes allow US hemp fibers to enter markets once dominated by petroleum-based synthetics, delivering performance without compromise.

## What Problems Are Being Solved For Hemp in India?

Hemp fiber is illegal to grow in many parts of the world, making import, distribution, manufacturing, and sale of the material complex. These challenges create an environment where it can be difficult to acquire hemp fabrics and yarns, mostly produced today throughout China and Europe.

The Heartland teams main focus is cutting red tape and opening access to sustainble US hemp fiber for brands throughout the world. Mitigating costs across the value chain and simplifying availability, we are able to commercially support full containers of finished fabrics and goods from India to US and EU made with US hemp fiber.

By creating the supply chain and establishing the benchmark for quality, we're inviting the world to embrace an entirely new fabric opportunity craved by consumers worldwide.
